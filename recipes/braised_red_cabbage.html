Grandpa Joe's Favorite Braised Red Cabbage

This is an excellent side dish for large holiday gatherings or a nice Sunday dinner. THe leftovers keep well for up to a week in the fridge. If you use apples that are naturally sweeter, you get a mostly sweet and savory dish. The sweetness of the apples will override the tartness of the apple cider vinegar.

Ingredients

  • 1 large head of red cabbage (roughly 6 cups chopped)
  • 1 large apple. sliced thinly
  • 1/2 red onion, sliced thinly
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vingear
  • 3 tbsp white sugar
  • 1/3 cup water
  • 2 cloves of garlic, finely minced
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Add all ingredients to a heavy bottomed pot. A 5 quart Dutch oven is recommended.
  2. Cover and cook on medium heat until the liquid starts to simmer. Turn the heat down to medium low and simmer for 90 minutes, stirring every 10-15 minutes to ensure even cooking.

Substitution notes

  • Balsamic vinegar can be used in place of apple cider vinegar. THe vinegar flavor will be more forward in the final dish.
  • If you love garlic, obviously add more garlic!
  • I prefer adding freshly ground black pepper at the end to taste, otherwise the flavor gets a bit lost during the cooking.

Dietary Accommodations

  • gluten-free
  • vegan
  • plant-based
  • kid-friendly
